A Branding for Japanese Performance Car’s Enthusiasts.
Japanese Domestic
Motor Revolution
Branding/Motion

JDM stands for "Japanese domestic market," it originally refers to the cars and vehicular parts that were designed and engineered to follow the road regulations in Japan. It is now more referring to the fact that car enthusiasts modified their Japanese brand vehicles based on the Japanese home market standard around the world.

JDMR (Japanese Domestic Motor Revolution) is a celebrating party that holds for Japanese performance car's enthusiasts, which helps JDM fans to gather together to share their car tunning knowledge and has fun to drive their customized JDM car instead of illegal street racing.

The main graphic elements are inspired by the "drift," which is a very popular stunt activity that JDM players always play with.

a BOOK ABOUT San francisco japantown.
The california rolls
in japantown
Book Design

Japantown is one of the famous landmarks in San Francisco
to attract tourists. It is the perfect place to discover the depth of culture of Japanese and Japanese American. However, my impression of Japantown was disappointing. Instead of reproducing the authentic Japanese culture and functioning as the window to peek japan, it is more like a Japanese theme park that squeezes everything that Americans think Japan should be to satisfy an exotic feeling.

Based on my days observing Japantown, I found that there are a lot of restaurants. For the execution of this book, I decided to do a book about food. The California Rolls is the most popular dish in the sushi restaurant around the world, and it is a representation of  Japanese American culture. Besides, the California Roll's price is affordable, and it tastes palatable. The book will talk about  the detail of the California Roll in SF Japantown.

An Augmented Reality Experience THAT DesignED for THE Art Exhibition at CCA.
Aug.art
Augmented Reality/Experience Design/Group Project

AUG•ART is an experience that combines Aurasma’s augmented
reality technology with traditional art forms to create unique, interactive, and informational experiences. Turning two dimensional works into 3D animations, Aurasma allows the viewer(s) to immerse themselves into the work one step further: Using the app, they can
gather information about the art, the artist, and the work’s under-lying concept, and can engage with a unique set of animations that bring the work to life. Through AUG•ART, ‘art’ and ‘collaboration’ become redefined; the piece takes on a multi-layered, multi-dimensional qualities.
